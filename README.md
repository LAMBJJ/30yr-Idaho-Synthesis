# 30yr-Idaho-Synthesis
A repository for the 30yrs+ of data from the Idaho Wild Fish project

## Primary Author 
Jesse J Lamb

### Coauthors/collaborators
Benjamin P. Sandford, Steven G. Smith, Aimee H. Fullerton, Loren W. Stearman, Gordon A. Axel, and Beth L. Sanderson

## Research Goals
- Characterize migration timing and growth and estimate parr-to-smolt survival to Lower Granite Dam for individual stream populations of wild Snake River spring/summer Chinook salmon
- Determine whether consistent patterns in migration timing and survival are apparent
- Determine which environmental factors may influence patterns in migration/survival
- Characterize the migrational behavior and estimated survival of different wild juvenile Chinook populations as they migrate from natal rearing areas

## Datasets
#### Idaho Wild Fish Juvenile Chinook Salmon PIT-Tagging Data
- Access: Draft assembled data are available on request from the authors, or via the associated [Github repository for the companion precocious parr project](URL). Data may also be extracted via the PIT Tag information system [(PTAGIS)](https://www.ptagis.org)
- Variables used: Tag code, abundance, length, and mass for all Chinook salmon parr, 1989 - 2024
- Additional variables used: Site characteristics and total sample area (m<sup>2</sup>)

#### PIT Tag Information System (PTAGIS)
- Access: [PTAGIS](https://www.ptagis.org)
- Specific queries/subsets: Queried for projects with codes SA (Steve Achord) or GAA (Gordon Axel), and for detections in HUC6 170602 (Salmon)
- Variables used: detections of Chinook salmon parr or smolts on in-stream arrays, rotary screw traps, and at Lower Granite Dam and Little Goose Dam, 1989 - 2024. 

#### Juvenile Fish Passage Facilty at Little Goose and Lower Granite Dams Separate-by-Code (SyC) Data
- Access: Available via queries on [PTAGIS](https://www.ptagis.org)
- Variables used: Tag code, length, and mass, for all recaptured Chinook salmon smolts, 2001-2011 (Little Goose), 2011-2024 (Lower Granite)

## Management/Mission Implications
- Describes new early life history diversity of Chinook salmonm, providing insights into how diversity might relate to population resiliency
- Provides more information on timing and patterns of outmigration, informing life cycle models and other demographic models with better parameters
- Synthesizes 30+ years of existing data to provide new insights into salmonid ecology
